By 2009, Wizkid signed a record deal with Empire Mates Entertainment. This deal drew the stage curtains wide open for the spotlight to be on Wizkid. You can see that he grabbed hold of that opportunity as his life depended on it. By 19, he co-wrote a song by Banky W titled “Omoge You Too Much”, which served as the beginning of his dealings with popular Nigerian artists. He also took his time to master the art of music, after he dropped out of school twice. Having had close to enough experience at hand, he started working on his debut studio album titled, ‘Superstar’ in 2009. He dropped the lead single off the album titled ‘Holla at Your Boy’ and his life never remained the same!! Immediately after the release of the lead single, it was nominated as the Best Pop Single, it also earned Wizkid the Next Rated Award at the Headies in 2011. The Video of the lead single also earned him the Award of the Most Gifted New Comer Video in 2011 at Channel O Music Video Awards. Having realised how good his craft was, Wizkid went on to release ‘Superstar‘ on June 12th 2011. Wizkid received a lot of awards for this album, but the greatest of all was the fame that came with it. He received both local and international recognition, which led him to tour London and perform at the HMV Apollo on 4th June 2012, as if that wasn’t enough, he went on to perform alongside Kendrick Lamar and others, in Brixton.
Growing up for Wizkid wasn’t as easy as a lot of people imagined it to be. In a radio interview with Tim Westwood, Wizkid stated that he grew up in a polygamous family that left him with 12 sisters and 2 stepmothers because his father married 3 wives. His inspiration for music came about when he was at the primary level of his education. He grew up listening to the likes of Fela Kuti, one of Nigeria’s, and Africa’s prominent political activists and Pan-Africanist who at that time was a popular singer as well. it is safe to state fact that Wizkid got a great part of his motivation from the Nigerian Icon. Wizkid dropped his first Album in 2001 with a group of children from his church, a group that he created and named the ‘Glorious five’. At that time, he was called ‘Lil Prinz’ and the album consisted of 7 tracks. This marked the beginning of his journey and at that time, there was no arguing the fact that he was going to become famous since it was clear; he had the talent and the willingness to let it shine!! Wizkid later changed his name from ‘Lil Prinz’ to ‘Wizkid’ in 2006 after he met a new producer that prevented him from recording a new song for some time.
